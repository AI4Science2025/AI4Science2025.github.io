I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Texas at Dallas, advised by Prof. Ovidiu Daescu. My research centers on machine learning for scientific discovery — building deep learning models for molecular property prediction (graph neural networks and transformers) and LLM-based multi-agent systems for scientific knowledge analysis. I have an interdisciplinary background spanning AI and biomedical research, with earlier work in glycobiology and immunology.

Research interests: Machine Learning for Science, Molecular Representation Learning, Graph Neural Networks, LLM Agents, and AI for Drug Discovery.

Research

BiScale-GTR: Multi-Scale Transformer–Graph Neural Network for Molecular Representation
Under review at TMLR · Oct 2024 – present
A parallel Transformer–GNN hybrid that models both long-range and local molecular interactions, with a graph-based tokenizer for reusable molecular substructures. Achieves state-of-the-art results on three molecular property prediction benchmarks.
Interpretable Machine Learning for Molecular Property Prediction
Published · Sep 2024 – Aug 2025
An end-to-end ML pipeline covering dataset curation, feature engineering, and explainable XGBoost modeling — reducing 2,000+ molecular descriptors to 10 key predictors with SHAP-based interpretability.
Literature-Grounded Limitation Detection via Cross-Paper Reasoning
Ongoing · Mar 2026 – present
An LLM-based multi-agent system for molecular modification and optimization grounded in the scientific literature.
